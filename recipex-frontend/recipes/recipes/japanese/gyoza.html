About

Gyoza are Japanese dumplings typically filled with ground pork and vegetables, pan-fried to achieve a crispy bottom and steamed to cook through. They're a popular appetizer or side dish in Japanese cuisine.

Video

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix ground pork, chopped cabbage, green onions, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and sesame oil until well combined.
  2. Place a small spoonful of filling in the center of each gyoza wrapper.
  3. Wet the edges of the wrapper with water and fold it in half, pleating the edges to seal tightly.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat.
  5. Place gyoza in the pan, flat side down, and cook until bottoms are golden brown (about 2-3 minutes).
  6. Add water to the pan, cover immediately, and steam for 5-6 minutes until the filling is cooked through.
  7. Remove the lid and cook for another 1-2 minutes to crisp the bottoms again. Serve hot.
