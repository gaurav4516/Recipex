About

Mochi is a traditional Japanese rice cake made by steaming sweet rice flour and then pounding it to a sticky, chewy texture. It can be enjoyed plain or filled with sweet red bean paste, making it a beloved dessert or snack.

Video

Steps

  1. Mix sweet rice flour, sugar, and water in a microwave-safe bowl until smooth.
  2. Cover loosely with plastic wrap and microwave on high for 2 minutes. Stir, then microwave for another 1-2 minutes until the mixture is sticky and translucent.
  3. Dust a clean surface with potato starch and transfer the mochi dough onto it.
  4. Dust your hands with starch and knead the mochi gently until smooth and no longer sticky.
  5. Divide the mochi into small pieces and flatten each piece into a disk.
  6. Place a small spoonful of red bean paste in the center, fold the edges to seal, and shape into a ball. Dust with more starch to prevent sticking. Serve fresh.
