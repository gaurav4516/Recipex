About

This authentic Goan fish curry features tender fish pieces simmered in a spicy-sour coconut-based gravy. Characterized by its vibrant red color from Kashmiri chilies and tangy flavor from kokum, it's traditionally served with steamed rice.

Video

Steps

  1. Soak red chilies in warm water for 20 minutes
  2. Grind soaked chilies, coconut, cumin, garlic and turmeric to smooth paste
  3. Heat coconut oil in earthen pot (or regular pan)
  4. Add mustard seeds and curry leaves for tempering
  5. Add ground masala paste and sauté for 5 minutes
  6. Add 2 cups water and bring to boil
  7. Stir in tamarind pulp and kokum pieces
  8. Add fish pieces gently to the boiling gravy
  9. Simmer on low heat for 8-10 minutes (don't stir vigorously)
  10. Pour in thick coconut milk and heat through (don't boil)
  11. Adjust salt and tanginess (add more kokum if needed)
  12. Let curry rest for 15 minutes for flavors to meld
  13. Finish with drizzle of raw coconut oil
  14. Serve hot with steamed rice or poi (Goan bread)
