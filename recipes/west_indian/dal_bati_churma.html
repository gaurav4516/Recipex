About

The iconic Rajasthani thali featuring three elements: Dal (spiced lentil curry), Baati (hard wheat rolls), and Churma (sweet wheat crumble). This traditional desert-state meal is characterized by its rich use of ghee and robust flavors.

Video

Steps

  1. Soak mixed lentils for 2 hours, pressure cook until soft
  2. For baati: Mix wheat flour, semolina, ¼ cup ghee and yogurt
  3. Knead into stiff dough, rest for 30 minutes
  4. Shape into round balls, make cross slit on top
  5. Bake at 180°C for 25-30 minutes until golden
  6. Dip hot baatis in ghee and keep aside
  7. For dal: Heat 2 tbsp ghee in kadhai
  8. Add cumin, asafoetida, chopped onions and sauté
  9. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates
  10. Mix in cooked lentils, turmeric, red chili powder
  11. Simmer for 15 minutes, finish with garam masala
  12. For churma: Bake wheat flour dough balls similar to baati
  13. Crumble baked balls into fine powder using mortar
  14. Mix with powdered jaggery, cardamom and chopped nuts
  15. Heat 2 tbsp ghee and mix into churma mixture
  16. To serve: Crush baati slightly, pour hot dal over it
  17. Serve with garlic chutney and onion salad
  18. Offer churma as sweet finale to the meal
